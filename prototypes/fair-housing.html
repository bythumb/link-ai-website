Fair Housing & Equal Opportunity

Updated May 15, 2025

Equal Housing Opportunity Link Management Systems and its affiliated brokerages are committed to equal housing opportunity for all persons regardless of protected class status.

Our Commitment

Link Management Systems, Inc. and all of its affiliated real estate brokerages, mortgage companies, and service entities are committed to the principles of the Fair Housing Act and the Equal Opportunity Act. We are dedicated to providing fair and equal housing opportunities to all individuals and are firmly opposed to any form of discrimination in housing.

We believe that every person has the right to choose where they live and that this right should be exercised free from discrimination, intimidation, or interference based on protected characteristics.

Protected Classes

Under federal law, it is illegal to discriminate in the sale, lease, or financing of housing or in the provision of brokerage services based on:

  • Race
  • Color
  • National Origin
  • Religion
  • Sex (including gender identity and sexual orientation under certain jurisdictions)
  • Familial Status (including children under 18 living with parents or legal custodians, pregnant women, and people securing custody of children under 18)
  • Disability (physical or mental)

Many states and localities extend protection to additional classes, including but not limited to: source of income, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity, age, ancestry, and military/veteran status. Link and its affiliates comply with all applicable state and local fair housing laws.

Our Obligations and Practices

Link and its affiliated brokerages and agents commit to:

  • Offering housing opportunities to all qualified persons without discrimination
  • Providing equal professional service to all clients and customers
  • Presenting all properties to all buyers, regardless of their background
  • Adhering to the National Association of REALTORS® Code of Ethics
  • Refraining from "steering" — directing clients toward or away from neighborhoods based on their protected class
  • Accurately representing available properties and their features to all prospects
  • Training all agents on fair housing laws, obligations, and best practices
  • Maintaining non-discriminatory advertising practices

Reasonable Accommodations and Modifications

Federal law requires landlords and housing providers to make reasonable accommodations in rules, policies, practices, and services when necessary for a person with a disability to have equal opportunity to use and enjoy housing. This includes:

  • Reasonable accommodations — changes in rules, policies, practices, or services
  • Reasonable modifications — physical changes to the unit or common areas (generally at the tenant's expense, unless the housing receives federal funding)

Requests for reasonable accommodations or modifications should be documented and responded to promptly. If you need an accommodation in connection with our services, please contact us.

Mortgage Lending — Equal Credit Opportunity

First Link Mortgage (NMLS #2527988) complies with the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) and the Fair Housing Act in all aspects of mortgage lending. We do not discriminate against any credit applicant on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age (provided the applicant has the legal capacity to enter into a contract), receipt of public assistance income, or the exercise of any right under the Consumer Credit Protection Act.

Notice Required by the Equal Credit Opportunity Act

The Federal Equal Credit Opportunity Act prohibits creditors from discriminating against credit applicants on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age (provided the applicant has the capacity to enter into a binding contract); because all or part of the applicant's income derives from any public assistance program; or because the applicant has in good faith exercised any right under the Consumer Credit Protection Act. The Federal agency that administers compliance with this law concerning this creditor is: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, 1700 G Street NW, Washington, DC 20552.

Filing a Fair Housing Complaint

If you believe you have experienced housing discrimination, you have the right to file a complaint. Complaints may be filed with:

  • U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) — online at hud.gov/fairhousing, by phone at 1-800-669-9777 (TTY: 1-800-927-9275), or by mail at HUD Office of Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity, 451 7th Street SW, Washington, DC 20410
  • Your state's civil rights or housing agency
  • A private attorney

You may also file a complaint directly with Link by contacting our compliance team. All complaints will be taken seriously and investigated promptly.
